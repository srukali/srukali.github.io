I'm Katelyn, a Digital Arts and Sciences (DAS) student at the University of Florida, a slave to my internet connection, and a fervent believer in the Oxford comma. My interests include Human-Computer Interaction, User Experience, Web Development, Mobile Development, Graphic Design, and Product Management. (Pretty much a little bit of everything.)

In case you were interested, DAS is a discipline where computer science and art intersect - the curriculum includes working with 3D modeling and animation software, Java GUI, immersion into video game development, and electives that let a student explore a greater breadth of artistic knowledge on design and aesthetics. I think it's a fantastic and unique program that offers me opportunities I wouldn't have found elsewhere.

In my spare time, I enjoy baking up some cupcakes, watching an episode of my latest favorite show on Netflix, or tending to my orchid that I'm determined to get blooming again. At night you may find me hanging out with my ball python Koa, or rather she may be hanging out on me.

In my class Interactive Processing, I chose to create a data visualization for the term project. This sketch was created to bring awareness to the difference of emotions displayed on Twitter in different geographical locations.

I served as the Product Manager in this scrum-based group project for Human Computer Interaction. We had to choose an interface that we wanted to improve, so we chose to improve the tedious text-entry process on the Xbox. My role was to evaluate the customer's needs and ensure that our final design was user-centered. After leading a focus group, I brought the user's concerns to my team and we developed a prototype. In our interface, the keyboard is broken up into 4 radial menus that are accessible from pressing a "hot key," or the left and right bumpers and triggers. To select a letter, the user holds a "hot key", rotates the left thumbstick to the desired letter, and releases.
